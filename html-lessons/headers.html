Headers and Paragraphs

What are headers?

A header looks like this in code

<h3></h3>

and

like this on a browser

. Headers are used to divide a page into sections, and give a clear definition as to the purpose of each page. So for example, I have used the

<h1></h1>

header tags at the top of the page, with the text 'Headers' to indicate that on this page we will be looking at header tags.

You may have noticed the different numbers appearing after the 'h' in these tags, that indicates the size of the heading.

<h1></h1>

is largest,

<h6></h6>

is smallest. Here is the difference in size.

What are paragraphs?

A paragraph tag looks like this

<p></p>

. They simply define a paragraph of text, such as this one you are reading right now. You have already seen an example of this and should be fairly familiar with the tag. It is a very common tag and should be used whenever you are writing blocks of text for a website.
